# Project Management Guidelines

## Overview

This document outlines the project management practices followed in our team.

## Key Practices
- Regularly communicate progress.
- Document decisions and discussions.
- Use project management tools effectively.

## Future Roadmap & Proposed Features

The following are potential narrative additions, interactive elements, and quality-of-life improvements that can be progressively integrated into the 3minsto9 ARG web experience. These ideas can be converted into actionable Kanban tasks or GitHub Issues.

### 1. Global ARG Progress / Status Page
*   **Concept:** A dynamic page tracking the "current state" of the conflict between Team Rabbit and MLTK.
*   **Implementation Ideas:**
    *   A dynamic puzzle progress bar.
    *   Cryptic "intercepted" messages that update over time (potentially utilizing a static JSON data file or future database integration).
    *   A countdown timer to a "major event" (like the titular "3 mins to 9").
*   **Narrative Value:** Creates urgency and shows players their collective progress against the corporate monolithic entity.

### 2. Expanded MLTK Corporate Intranet
*   **Concept:** Deepening the in-universe corporate side with more restrictive and satirical content.
*   **Implementation Ideas:**
    *   **Staff Directory:** Contains hidden clues or passwords for specific employees (like Gretchen).
    *   **System Diagnostics/Terminal:** Interactive mini-games mimicking terminal access.
    *   **Incident Reports:** Narrative documents providing world-building context on "breaches" (Team Rabbit activities).
*   **Narrative Value:** Reinforces the oppressive, bureaucratic nature of MLTK while hiding key lore.

### 3. Team Rabbit Safehouse / Comm Node
*   **Concept:** A hidden, secure section of the site acting as a rebel hub.
*   **Implementation Ideas:**
    *   Accessed via the `radio_scanner_utils.js` (entering a specific frequency) or hidden links on the Surface page.
    *   Features a secure chat room, file drop (for player submissions/evidence), or mission briefing board.
*   **Narrative Value:** Provides a direct line of communication from the "underground" to the players, assigning tasks and providing narrative updates.

### 4. Interactive "Hacking" Minigames
*   **Concept:** Small, self-contained puzzles embedded within the MLTK pages.
*   **Implementation Ideas:**
    *   Simulated terminal logins requiring brute-forcing or cipher decoding.
    *   Sequence matching games to bypass "security firewalls."
*   **Narrative Value:** Turns passive reading into active engagement, making players feel like genuine operatives working against MLTK.

### 5. Lore Wiki / "The Story So Far" Archive
*   **Concept:** An out-of-character or diegetic summary page to help onboard new players.
*   **Implementation Ideas:**
    *   A chronological timeline of events.
    *   Profiles of key characters and entities.
    *   Summaries of solved puzzles and their outcomes.
*   **Narrative Value:** Lowers the barrier to entry for latecomers to the ARG, ensuring they can understand the context without needing to read months of backlogs.
